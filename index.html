About me

Since 2019 I am a postdoctoral researcher in the GC department of the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy (MPIA) in Heidelberg, Germany. I obtained my PhD in Leiden (the Netherlands) under the supervision of Anthony Brown and Tim de Zeeuw (you can access my PhD thesis here). A PDF with my CV can be found here.

My current research focuses on young stars, both luminous high-mass O- and B-type stars and low-mass pre-main sequence stars. I leverage on the complementarity of the information provided by OB and PMS stars to map star formation in the Milky Way and obtain a detailed view of the structure and star formation history of the young disc of our Galaxy. I am also actively involved in the SDSS-V survey, where I lead the program focused on observing massive stars. You can view my publications in ADS here.

Mapping Luminous Hot Stars in the Milky Way

Zari, E., Rix, H.-W., Frankel, N., et al. (2021)

We map the spatial distribution of a O- and B-type stars across the Galactic disc, and find it to be highly structured, with distinct over- and under-densities. The spatial picture of the Milky Way's young disc structure is complex, and suggests that most young stars in our Galaxy are not neatly organised into distinct spiral arms.

3D mapping of young stars in the solar neighbourhood

Zari, E., Hashemi, H., Brown, A.G.A., et al. (2018)

We study the three dimensional arrangement of young stars in the solar neighbourhood using the second release of the Gaia mission (Gaia DR2) and we provide a new, original view of the spatial configuration of the star-forming regions within 500 pc of the Sun. The youngest groups that we find are mainly located in Scorpius-Centaurus, Orion, Vela, and Taurus. Cepheus, Cassiopeia, and Lacerta are instead more evolved and less numerous. The 3D density maps show no evidence for the existence of the ring-like structure which is usually referred to as the Gould Belt.

Structure, kinematics, and ages of the young stellar populations in the Orion region

Zari, E., Brown, A.G.A., de Zeeuw, P.T. (2019)

We present a study of the three dimensional structure, kinematics, and age distribution of the Orion OB association, based on the second data release of the Gaia satellite (Gaia DR2). Our findings suggest that star formation in Orion does not follow a simple sequential scenario, but instead consists of multiple events, which caused kinematic and physical sub-structure.
